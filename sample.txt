KING ALARIC:
The moon is pale above the silent tower,
And every stone remembers what we lost.

LADY ELENA:
My lord, the night is full of restless wind.
It speaks as though the dead would rise again.

KING ALARIC:
Then let them rise, if truth must walk with ghosts.
I fear no shadow born from honest grief.

CAPTAIN ROWAN:
The gates are locked, the watchmen hold their posts,
Yet still the western road is stained with fire.

LADY ELENA:
Who comes so late beneath a blood-red sky?

CAPTAIN ROWAN:
A rider, mad with haste, from Elden Field.
His horse is torn, his cloak is black with rain.

MESSENGER:
My king, forgive the breath I spend so poorly.
The northern bridge has fallen to the sea.

KING ALARIC:
The bridge? Speak plainly, man. Who struck the stones?

MESSENGER:
No hand of man, my lord, but thunder's wrath.
The river rose like iron in the dark.

LADY ELENA:
Then all our soldiers stand beyond our reach.

KING ALARIC:
Not beyond courage. Not beyond command.
Send word by raven, torch, and temple bell.

CAPTAIN ROWAN:
The men are weary, yet their hearts are yours.

KING ALARIC:
A weary heart may still defend a crown.
A broken road may still remember home.

LADY ELENA:
You speak of crowns, but I have seen your eyes.
You mourn the boys who marched at dawn today.

KING ALARIC:
A king who cannot mourn is made of stone.
A king who only mourns will lose the realm.

MESSENGER:
There is more news, though fear has chained my tongue.

KING ALARIC:
Break every chain. The truth is welcome here.

MESSENGER:
Prince Adrian rides alone toward the pass.

LADY ELENA:
Alone? He is but young in war's cruel book.

CAPTAIN ROWAN:
Give me ten riders and the fastest steeds.
I will return him before the morning bell.

KING ALARIC:
Go then, good Rowan. Let the torches fly.
If dawn must come with sorrow, let it find
That we were not asleep when darkness called.

LADY ELENA:
And if the prince is lost?

KING ALARIC:
Then I shall seek him
Wherever grief can travel, earth or heaven.

CAPTAIN ROWAN:
My sword is sworn.

KING ALARIC:
And so is mine, old friend.
Ride hard. The kingdom listens for your hooves.

LADY ELENA:
The hall grows colder when brave men depart.

KING ALARIC:
Then light the fires. Let every window burn.
Tonight we teach the darkness where we stand.

CHORUS:
So rings the bell above the ancient keep,
While rain and rumor cross the wounded land.
Some hearts are crowns, and some are shields of sleep,
And some are candles trembling in the hand.
